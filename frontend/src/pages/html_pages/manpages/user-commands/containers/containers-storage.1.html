A container is essentially a layer which is a child  of  a  layer
which is referred to by an image (put another way, a container is
instantiated   from   an  image),  along  with  some  bookkeeping
information.  They do not have children and their layers can  not
be  directly referred to by images.  This ensures that changes to
the contents of a container’s layer do not affect other images or
layers, so they are considered writeable.
