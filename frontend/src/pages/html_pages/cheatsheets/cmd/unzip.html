Extract Files from Archives

The unzip command is a powerful utility for extracting files from compressed archives, most commonly in the ZIP format. This tool is essential for managing compressed data, whether you're downloading software, sharing files, or organizing your projects. Our online Unzip tool provides a user-friendly interface to perform these operations without needing to install any software.

Common Unzip Commands

Here are some of the most frequently used commands for the unzip utility:

---
tags: [ compression, archive ]
---
# To extract an archive:
unzip <archive>

# To extract an archive to a specific directory:
unzip <archive> -d <directory>

# To test integrity of archive:
unzip -tq <archive>

# To list files and directories an archive:
unzip -l <archive>

Understanding Archive Formats

While unzip is primarily associated with ZIP files, it can sometimes handle other archive formats depending on the system's configuration.
